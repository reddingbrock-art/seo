What's Included

How the Review System Works for Pest Control Companies

▶

Satisfaction Check Fires First — Every Time

After a job closes in your pipeline, the system automatically sends a short satisfaction check to the customer before any review request goes out. This is the gatekeeper. It makes sure you know how the customer feels before they're ever pointed toward Google. No review link gets sent until this step is complete.

▶

Positive Responses Go to Google — Negative Ones Come to You

When a customer responds positively to the satisfaction check, they immediately receive a direct link to leave a Google review. When someone responds negatively, they're routed to a private feedback form that goes straight to you — the owner. That feedback never becomes a public review. Both paths are part of the same mechanism, running automatically from a single trigger.

▶

Reviews Accumulate From Every Job Without the Awkward Ask

Your techs don't have to bring it up. You don't have to remember to send anything. Every completed job triggers the sequence, which means reviews build consistently from your actual volume of work. Over a busy Dallas summer, that compounds fast — without anyone on your team doing anything different.

▶

Timing Matters — The Request Hits While the Work Is Fresh

The automation fires right after job close, when the customer still remembers the tech's name and the results they just saw. A review request that lands two days later gets ignored. One that arrives while the customer is still looking at their clean baseboards or checking the bait stations? That one gets action.
