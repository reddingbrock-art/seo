What's Included

How the Two-Part Review System Works for Electrical Companies

▶

Satisfaction Check Fires First — Every Time

The moment a job is marked complete in your pipeline, a satisfaction check goes out to the customer automatically. This always happens before any review link is sent. It's a quick, friendly message asking how the work went. Think of it as your quality control gate — nothing public happens until you know where the customer stands.

▶

Positive Feedback Goes to Google, Negative Stays Private

When a customer responds positively to the satisfaction check, they immediately receive a direct link to leave a Google review. If they respond with a concern or complaint, they're routed to a private feedback form that comes straight to you — the owner. That negative feedback never touches Google. Both paths are part of one mechanism, and they fire based on the customer's own response.

▶

Reviews Accumulate Without Awkward Conversations

Your techs finish the job and move on. Your office staff doesn't have to remember to send anything. Every completed job triggers the sequence automatically, which means reviews build steadily from your actual volume of work. No clipboard at the door, no "hey, could you leave us a review" while the customer's signing the invoice.

▶

Timing That Actually Gets Responses

The request fires right after job close, while the customer still remembers your tech by name and the lights are working perfectly. Wait two days and you've lost them. This system catches people in that narrow window when they're most willing to take thirty seconds and leave a review. That timing alone makes a significant difference in response rates.
